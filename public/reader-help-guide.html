Reader Help Guide

A walkthrough of the Screenplay Readers staff portal — everything you need to manage your assignments from start to finish.

Welcome

The Screenplay Readers staff portal is where you manage your coverage work from start to finish. Once you're logged in you can browse available scripts, accept assignments, read the material, submit completed coverage, and track your pay — all in one place.

This guide covers everything you need to work confidently in the portal. If something isn't covered here, reach out to your editor or admin directly.

Logging In

Navigate to the portal URL provided by your editor and sign in with your email address and password.

ℹ️
First time logging in?

Your account is created by an admin. You'll receive an invitation email with a link to set your password. If that link has expired, use the Forgot your password? link on the login page to generate a new one.

If you forget your password at any time, use the Forgot your password? link on the login screen. A reset link will be emailed to you.

Assignments Dashboard

After logging in you'll land on the Assignments page. This is your home base — everything you need to do your work is here.

The Staff Panel

At the top of the page you'll see a row of small circular avatar icons. This is the staff panel. It shows you who is currently active in the portal, including editors, admins, and other readers.

  • Indigo icons are editors and admins. Gray icons are readers.
  • A green dot in the bottom-right corner of an icon means that person is currently active in the portal (within the last 5 minutes).
  • A dashed red border around a reader's icon means they have marked themselves as unavailable.

  • The panel refreshes automatically every 5 minutes.

The Three Tabs

Below the staff panel are three tabs that organize your work:

Tab What it shows
Available Assignments Scripts open for acceptance — either available to any reader, or assignments where a customer has specifically requested you.
My Assignments Assignments you've accepted that are currently in progress.
Needs Attention Assignments flagged by your editor that require action from you before they can be delivered. An orange alert banner appears at the top of the page when you have items here.
⚠️
Watch the Needs Attention tab.

If an assignment appears here, your editor has reviewed your coverage and needs revisions before it can be delivered to the customer. Address these promptly.

Reading the Assignment List

Each row in the Available Assignments list shows the key details you need before accepting:

  • Script title and writer name
  • Assignment type — e.g. Script Coverage, Notes-Only, Deep-Dive, Short
  • Page count and pay rate — shown as Xp · $XX.XX
  • Age — how long the assignment has been waiting. The color shifts from green → yellow → orange → red as it ages, so you can see at a glance which orders have been sitting longest.
  • Rush — rush assignments show an amber badge and a flashing "Due by…" deadline. These must be submitted within 23 hours of the order timestamp.
  • Reader Request — a small purple icon next to a reader's initials indicates a customer specifically requested that reader for this script.

Click anywhere on a row to open the full assignment detail.

Accepting an Assignment

Step by Step

  1. Browse the Available Assignments tab.

    Look through the list and find a script you'd like to cover. Check the type, page count, pay rate, and any deadline before committing.

  2. Click the row to open the assignment.

    The detail view shows all information about the script and the order, including the full pay breakdown.

  3. Click Accept.

    Click the Accept Assignment button. The assignment will immediately move to your My Assignments tab and its status will change to Assigned.

  4. Read the script.

    The script title in your My Assignments list is a clickable link that opens the script in an inline viewer. You can scroll through it, jump to any page by dragging the scrollbar, or download it if you prefer to read offline.

Reader Requests

Customers can request a specific reader when placing their order. If a customer has requested you, the assignment appears in your Available Assignments tab with a small purple icon labeled Request. Reader-requested assignments are only visible to you and to admins/editors — other readers cannot see or accept them.

✅
Reader Requests don't count toward your maximum.

Even if you're at your concurrent assignment limit, you can still accept a script where a customer has specifically requested you.

Rush Orders

Rush orders are marked with an Rush badge. The "Due by…" text flashes in orange to draw your attention to the deadline. Rush orders must be submitted within 23 hours of the order timestamp shown on the row.

Rush orders carry a higher pay rate to reflect the fast turnaround. Like reader requests, rush orders do not count toward your maximum concurrent assignments.

Capacity Limit

Your profile includes a maximum concurrent assignments setting (configured by your editor). When you're at your limit, the Accept button will not appear on standard assignments — you'll need to complete or cancel an existing one first to free up a slot.

Two types of assignments bypass the limit entirely:

  • Reader-requested assignments (customer specifically asked for you)
  • Rush orders

Cancelling an Assignment

If you've accepted an assignment and need to give it back, you can cancel it from the assignment detail page as long as its status is still Assigned. Click Cancel Assignment and confirm the prompt. The assignment returns to the available pool for other readers.

⚠️
You cannot cancel after submitting coverage.

Once coverage is submitted, the assignment moves to QC and cancellation is no longer available. Contact your editor if you need help at that point.

Writing Coverage

Opening the Coverage Form

Once you've accepted an assignment and read the script, navigate to your My Assignments tab and locate the assignment. Click the Write Coverage button on the right side of the row to open the coverage form.

The Coverage Form

Work through each section carefully. The fields you'll see vary slightly by assignment type, but typically include:

  • Logline — One or two sentences summarising the script's premise.
  • Synopsis — A thorough summary of the story, usually scene-by-scene or act-by-act.
  • Comments — Your analysis of the craft: structure, character, dialogue, theme, marketability, and any other elements relevant to the assignment type.
  • Ratings / Scores — Numerical or categorical evaluations where required.
  • Recommendation — Your overall verdict (e.g. Recommend, Consider, Pass).
✅
You don't have to finish in one sitting.

Your work is saved as you go. You can return to the coverage form at any time before final submission and pick up where you left off.

Submitting Your Coverage

  1. Review everything before submitting.

    Read back through your coverage. Check for spelling, completeness, and that all required fields are filled in.

  2. Click Submit Coverage.

    Once submitted the assignment status changes to QC. Your editor will review it before it's delivered to the customer.

  3. Watch for Needs Attention.

    If your editor needs revisions, the assignment will reappear in your Needs Attention tab with feedback notes. Address the feedback and re-submit — the assignment will go back through QC.

ℹ️
Submission is not instant delivery.

All coverage goes through a QC review by your editor before it reaches the customer. The QC status means it's in your editor's queue — no action needed from you unless it comes back as Needs Attention.

Assignment Statuses

Every assignment moves through a series of statuses. Here's what each one means from your perspective:

Status What it means Action needed?
Pending A new order has just arrived and is being set up. Not yet in the available list. None
Available The assignment is open and ready to be accepted by a reader. Accept it if you're interested
Assigned You've accepted the assignment and are working on coverage. Write and submit coverage
Needs Attention Your editor reviewed your coverage and needs changes before delivery. Yes — review feedback and resubmit
QC You've submitted coverage. Your editor is reviewing it. None — wait for the QC outcome
Completed Coverage approved and delivered to the customer. Assignment is done. None
Cancelled The assignment was cancelled — by you, an editor, or admin. None

Getting Paid

How Pay is Calculated

Each assignment displays its pay rate in the list view as Xp · $XX.XX. The rate is calculated when the assignment is created and is based on several factors:

Factor Effect on pay
Assignment type Each type (Script Coverage, Notes-Only, Deep-Dive, Short, etc.) has its own base rate.
Page count Scripts over 120 pages carry an oversized surcharge.
Rush A rush fee is added on top of the base rate for 23-hour turnaround orders.
Reader Request A request fee is added when a customer has specifically requested you. Exception: Deep-Dive Dev Notes orders always include a free reader request — no fee is added.

You can view the full current rate breakdown on the Rates page (see the Rates section below).

Payment Method

Payment is issued via PayPal. Make sure your PayPal email address is correctly entered in your profile. Navigate to Profile from the top navigation, update the PayPal email field, and save.

⚠️
Double-check your PayPal email before your first payment.

Payments go directly to the address on file. If it's wrong your payment may go to the wrong account or bounce entirely. Contact your editor immediately if you need to correct an email after a payment has been sent.

Pay Schedule

Pay is processed by your editor or admin after your coverage has passed QC and the assignment is marked Completed. Your editor will communicate the specific payment cadence (weekly, bi-weekly, etc.).

Your Profile

Access your profile by clicking your name or the Profile link in the navigation. Keep this information current — it affects how assignments are routed to you and how you get paid.

Availability

Your availability status signals to editors and admins whether you're open to new work.

Setting What it does
Available You're open for assignments. Your icon in the staff panel appears normally.
Unavailable You're not accepting new work. Your icon shows a dashed red border, signalling to admins and editors that you're currently out.

Toggle your availability whenever you go on holiday, take a break, or come back from one. Your editor can see your status in the staff panel in real time — no need to message them separately.

Display Name and Initials

Your display name appears in assignment records and pay reports. Your initials (up to 3 characters) are what appear on your avatar icon throughout the portal. Make sure both are set correctly so your editor can identify your work easily.

Profile Photo

You can upload a profile photo, which replaces your initials on your avatar icon across the portal. A square crop works best. Keep the file size reasonable — it's displayed small.

Maximum Concurrent Assignments

This is set by your editor and controls how many assignments you can hold at the same time. You cannot change this yourself — contact your editor if you'd like to adjust your cap up or down.

✅
Rush orders and Reader Requests are exempt from your cap.

Even at your maximum, you can still accept these. Factor that in if you tend to receive frequent reader requests.

PayPal Email

Enter the email address linked to your PayPal account. This is where your pay will be sent. Set it before your first assignment is completed.

Rates

The Rates page is available from the main navigation. It shows the current pay rates for all assignment types so you always know what you'll earn before accepting a script.

The rates page includes:

  • Base rate per assignment type
  • Oversized page surcharge (scripts over 120 pages)
  • Rush order fee
  • Reader Request fee
ℹ️
Deep-Dive Dev Notes include a free Reader Request.

When you're assigned to a Deep-Dive Dev Notes order that carries a reader request, no request fee is added to your pay rate — it's already included in the service price.

FAQ

An assignment was mentioned to me but I can't see it in the available list. What's going on?

The assignment may still be in Pending status — it just arrived from the customer and hasn't been moved to Available yet. It's also possible the assignment carries a reader request for a different reader and isn't visible to you. Ask your editor to confirm its status.

The Accept button isn't showing on an assignment. Why?

You're most likely at your maximum concurrent assignment limit. Complete or cancel one of your current assignments to free up a slot. If the assignment has a reader request for another reader, you won't be able to accept it regardless of your capacity.

I submitted coverage but the assignment is still showing in My Assignments.

After submission the assignment moves to QC status. It remains visible in your My Assignments tab so you can monitor its progress. It will be marked Completed once your editor has approved and delivered it.

My coverage was sent back. What do I do?

Go to the Needs Attention tab. Open the assignment and read the notes from your editor. Return to the coverage form, make the requested changes, and re-submit. The assignment will go back into QC review.

The script viewer is loading slowly. Is there a way to jump to a specific page?

Yes — you can drag the scrollbar directly to jump to any position in the document instantly rather than scrolling page by page.

Can I see assignments I completed in the past?

Past completed assignments aren't surfaced in the reader view. Contact your editor if you need a record of your completed work or pay history.

How do I know if an editor or admin is available to answer a question?

Check the staff panel at the top of the Assignments page. Editors and admins with a green dot on their icon are currently active in the portal.

I need to take time off. What should I do?

Set your availability to Unavailable in your profile. Your icon will immediately show the dashed red border in the staff panel so your editor knows you're out. Switch it back to Available when you return.

My pay rate on an assignment looks wrong. Who do I contact?

Check the Rates page to verify what the rate should be, then reach out to your editor with the order number and the discrepancy. Rates are set when the assignment is created and can be corrected by an admin before payment is issued.
